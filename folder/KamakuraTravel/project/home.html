About Kamakura

Kamakura is a seaside Japanese city just south of Tokyo. The political center of medieval Japan, modern-day Kamakura is a prominent resort town with dozens of Buddhist Zen temples and Shinto shrines. Its most recognizable landmark is the Kotoku-in Temple’s Great Buddha, a roughly 13m-high bronze statue still standing after a 15th-century tsunami. Yuigahama Beach on Sagami Bay is a popular surfing spot

Kamakura's model itinerary

If you want to enjoy the main sightseeing spots in Kamakura in 1 day, we recommend the following route.

  • 【9:30】From Kamakura station, we will go around Tsuruoka Hachimangu and Komachi.

    After visiting Tsuruoka Hachimangu Shrine, you can walk through Komachi Street on your way back. There are many snacks in Komachi Street, so you can buy and eat while walking. Eat a lot of food because there are many places to go afterwards.

    • Tsuruoka Hachimangu Shrine

  • 【11:30】At the Hokokuji，Through the mysterious bamboo forest

    "There are more than 2,000 bamboos growing in the temple, which is also called "Bamboo Temple". It attracts more and more visitors.

    It takes about 12 minutes by bus from Kamakura Station to Kotokuji Temple. On foot, it takes about 30 minutes.

    • Bamboo Temple

  • 【13:00】Lunch at "Akimoto" near Kamakura Station

    In front of Kamakura Station“Akimoto”It is a good place to have lunch. It is famous for its dishes made with the famous local vegetables.

    • Akimoto

  • 【14:00】Moving by Enoshima Electric Railway, from NagoyaStation hiking to Hasedera and Kōtoku-in

    From Kamakura Station, you can use Enoshima Electric Railway (hereinafter referred to as Enoden). We recommend purchasing a "(のりおりくん)” ticket. Adults 600 yen, children 300 yen. You can get on and off the train at any station throughout the day, so you can use it again and again, which is very economical.

    From Kamakura Station to Hase Station, which is three stations away, it is a five-minute walk from Hase Station to Hasedera, where hydrangeas bloom in June and red leaves in late October, making it colorful almost all year round.

    Then walk for another 5 minutes and check out Kōtoku-in.

    • Kōtoku-in

  • 【16:00】A classic venue in Japanese anime, Kamakura High School Mae Station

    After visiting the two representative temples in Kamakura, return to Hase Station and take the Enoden train to Fujisawa and get off at Kamakura High School Station after 4 stops. Taking pictures here is something that everyone does. It is not to be missed for pilgrimage partners. After that, take 2 stops towards Kamakura and go to Inamura Gasaki Station. The sunset here is breathtakingly beautiful, and if the weather is good, you can see Mount Fuji.

    • Kamakura High School Mae Station
